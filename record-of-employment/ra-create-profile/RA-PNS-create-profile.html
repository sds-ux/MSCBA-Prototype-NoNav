Privacy Statement

You may want to print this page for future reference, since it contains important information.

Protecting your personal information and privacy is important to us.

Collecting, using and disclosing your personal information in relation to these online services is authorized by the Employment Insurance Act, the Department of Human Resources and Skills Development Act and the Privacy Act.

The information you provide when creating your ROE Web account and when using the ROE Web application is collected by the Canada Employment Insurance Commission (Commission) under the authority of the Employment Insurance Act for the purpose of its administration and enforcement. Completion is voluntary; however refusal to provide the personal information will result in not obtaining access to the ROE Web System.

The information you provide may also be used or disclosed for policy analysis, research evaluation purposes and may be linked to information under the control of the Commission. In some cases, information may be disclosed without your consent as authorized by law, including sharing with the Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP) the information pertaining to the employer and reason of layoff for the purpose of the TFWP administration and enforcement of the Labour Market Impact Assessment.

The information you provide will be administered in accordance with the Employment Insurance Act, the Department of Human Resources and Skills Development Act, the Privacy Act and other applicable laws. Under the Privacy Act, you have the right to the protection of, access to, and correction of your personal information, which is described in Personal Information Banks: HRSDC PPU 150, 180 and 385. Instructions for making formal requests about your personal information are outlined in the government publication entitled Info Source, which is available at the following web site address: www.infosource.gc.ca. Info Source may also be accessed on-line at any Service Canada Centre.

Completion of the Record of Employment is mandatory whenever there is an interruption of earnings. Failure to issue an ROE within the time limit is a breach of Section 136 of the Employment Insurance Act. On conviction, the employer may be subject to a fine or imprisonment or both.
