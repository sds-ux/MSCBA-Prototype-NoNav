Online Agreement

ROE WEB Agreement

The ROE Web Agreement (herein after "Agreement") governs the terms and conditions pursuant to which the Account Holder through its User(s) will have access to the ROE Web service for the purpose of completing and submitting electronic Records of Employment ("ROEs") to the Canada Employment Insurance Commission (Commission).

The purpose of ROE Web is to provide Account Holders and User(s) with a means to create, submit, print, view, extract and amend (all of which are hereinafter referred to as "manage") electronic ROEs.

This Agreement contains a number of provisions that may affect the rights and obligations of the Account Holder.

1. Definitions

a.obligated to complete and submit ROEs to the Commission in accordance with the Employment Insurance Act and Regulations; and/or

b. that is a Service Provider responsible for completing and submitting ROEs to the Commission on behalf of a Client Employer whether or not that Client Employer is registered with ROE Web as an Account Holder.

"Authorization Code" is a random, unique and temporary system generated code that the Account Holder will give to the Primary Officer (PO) in order for the PO to complete the registration to ROE Web.

"Client Employer" means the individual or entity that has authorized a Service Provider to manage ROEs on its behalf in accordance with the ROE Web Client Employer Consent Form.

"Main Business Address" means the Account Holder's business address as specified in the Account Holder ROE Web Account.

"Primary Officer" is an individual who has been designated by the Account Holder to act on its behalf and whose responsibilities are to (a) identify and authenticate other individual(s) as Representatives(s), (b) administer the Account Holder's ROE Web account, (c) accept the ROE Web agreement on behalf of the Account Holder, including amendments, and (d) manage ROEs.

"Record of Employment" for the purpose of ROE Web, is the electronic form, available for completion within ROE Web, which is completed in respect of a person employed in insurable employment who has an interruption of earnings.

"Representative" is an individual who has been identified and authenticated either by the Primary Officer or another Representative with appropriate access level. Representatives' responsibilities vary as per the access level assigned to them within the ROE Web.

"ROE Web Account" is the allotted space within ROE Web that the Account Holder can use for the purpose set out in this Agreement. An ROE Web account is comprised of one agreement, one Primary Officer, may have many Representatives and may have multiple Canada Revenue Agency Business Numbers in relation to which ROEs are managed by the Account Holder.

"ROE Web Client Employer Consent Form" is the form supplied by the Commission and used to confirm a Service Provider's authority to manage ROEs on behalf of a Client Employer.

"ROE Web Enrolment" is the process by which an individual is granted access levels to an ROE Web Account as a User.

"Service Provider" means an individual or entity that (a) provides accounting and payroll services to its Client Employer(s), (b) is authorized by its Client Employer(s) to manage ROEs on their behalf and in accordance with the ROE Web Client Employer Consent Form.

"User(s)" is the generic term referring to the individual(s) authorized to act on behalf of the Account Holder either as a Primary Officer or as a Representative.

"You" refers to the Primary Officer.

ROE Web is free of charge.

6. Responsibilities of the Account Holder

6.1 The Account Holder is fully responsible for

a. any use of ROE Web that is in breach of this Agreement, including the use by any User(s) to whom access to the ROE Web Account is granted;

b. ensuring that all information provided to the Commission through the ROE Web Account is true, accurate, current, complete and in compliance with the Employment Insurance Act and Regulations;

c. implementing processes or mechanisms supporting accurate validation of the identity of each User prior to completion of the ROE Web Enrolment. Such processes or mechanisms will be provided to the Commission upon request.

6.2 The Account Holder certifies that the Primary Officer and the Representatives(s) having been granted the appropriate access level, are authorized to use the ROE Web Account and to identify and authenticate additional User(s) to act on behalf of the Account Holder for the purpose of ROE Web.

6.3 The Account Holder certifies and warrants that

a. identity information provided by User(s) for purposes of ROE Web Enrolment is accurate and complete;

b. in seeking enrolment to ROE Web for its User(s), that it has no information or knowledge that the identity information of these Users as provided to the Commission during the enrolment process is inaccurate, incomplete, has changed or has been altered, and;

c. Users are authorized to be enrolled and use ROE Web in accordance with the access level assigned to them.

6.4 The Account Holder must notify the Commission if it knows or suspects that

a. any User's username or password has been or may have been disclosed to anyone;

b. any User's username, password or account has been or may have been accessed or used without authorization;

c. any provision of this Agreement has been or may have been contravened whether by act or omission;

d. any breach of security that impacts or may impact on the security or integrity of ROE Web has occurred.

6.5. In accordance with the process established by the Commission and found on the Service Canada website, the Account Holder must

a. notify the Commission of any change of Primary Officer; and

b. have the incoming Primary Officer identified and authenticated.

7. Responsibilities specific to the Account Holder acting as a Service Provider

As a Service Provider, the Account Holder

a. must keep, for audit purposes, the original of each ROE Web Client Employer Consent Form signed by a Client Employer authorizing the Account Holder to manage ROEs on their behalf using ROE Web. Such records will be maintained in accordance with the retention period provided for under subsections 87(3) and 87(3.1) of the Employment Insurance Act;

b. must, upon request, provide the Commission with a copy of the signed ROE Web Client Employer Consent Form in regard to each Client Employer, identified in the request, who authorizes the Account Holder to manage ROEs on the Client Employer's behalf ;

c. must cease to manage ROEs on behalf of a Client Employer who has terminated their authorization and must immediately inform the Commission of said termination;

d. understands and agrees that a Client Employer may, at any time, notify the Commission that they have terminated their authorization and that the Account Holder is no longer authorized to manage ROEs on their behalf.

8. Accuracy

The Account Holder understands and agrees that unless the information contained within ROE Web is updated or the Commission is notified of any change, the Commission is entitled to rely on and act upon the information it receives through ROE Web in relation to the Account Holder's account.

9. Protection of Username and Password

9.1 Subject to the terms of this Agreement, the Authorization Code assigned to the Primary Officer is personal and not transferable.

9.2 The Account Holder must ensure that prior to each User ROE Web Enrolment, Users understand and agree that their username and password used to access ROE Web are unique to them, and that they must maintain the confidentiality of their username and password at all times and must not let any other person use their username and password in order to preserve the security of their electronic communications.

9.3 The Commission disclaims all liability for any loss or damage arising from the failure by the Account Holder or any User(s) to protect usernames, passwords, Authorization Codes or account information, including the Account Holder's failure to meet its obligations under section 9.2.

10. Protection of Account Holder's Computer Networks

The Account Holder warrants that it will install, use and regularly update antivirus software and conduct malicious code scans on all computer equipment used to access ROE Web and further warrants that it will install new virus definitions as soon as practical.

11. Revocation of Enrolment to ROE Web

11.1 The Account Holder must revoke a User's access to the ROE Web Account when

a. the individual ceases to be a User of the ROE Web Account;

b. there is any change in the User's identification or authentication information;

c. the User's username or password is compromised or insecure, or suspected of being compromised or insecure;

d. the User fails to meet their obligations under this Agreement.

11.2 For greater certainty, the revocation of a User's access to the ROE Web Account does not affect the access of other Users, and does not terminate this Agreement. If the Account Holder revokes the access of the Primary Officer from ROE Web, the Account Holder must replace the Primary Officer and inform the Commission of the new Primary Officer as per section 6.5 of this Agreement.

11.3 The Account Holder must inform Users that their access to the ROE Web Account will be revoked where there is reason to believe that any of the circumstances set out in section 11.1 occur.

11.4 The Commission may, in its sole and unfettered discretion and without prior notice, revoke a User's access to ROE Web where there is reason to believe that any of the circumstances set out in section 11.1 occur.
